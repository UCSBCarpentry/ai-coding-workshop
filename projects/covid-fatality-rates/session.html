Here is a comprehensive description and detailed column-by-column analysis of the downloaded COVID-19 US County dataset.

General Description of the Dataset

The USCounties_cases.csv dataset contains 3,334 rows and 93 columns. It is a comprehensive, multi-dimensional dataset that blends COVID-19 epidemiological metrics (at both county and state levels) with demographic, socioeconomic, healthcare capacity, and policy policy/intervention data across United States counties and territories.

The epidemiological tracking date for most records is March 10, 2023 (near the sunset of the COVID-19 national emergency in the US). In addition to physical counties, the dataset includes administrative placeholders like “Out of [State]” or “Unassigned” to capture cases and deaths not explicitly attributed to a specific county.

Detailed Column Dictionary

To make the 93 columns digestible, they are categorized into 9 thematic groups:

1. Administrative & Geographic Identifiers

  • OBJECTID: A unique row identifier / database index.
  • Countyname: Name of the county or territory (e.g., Autauga, Baldwin).
  • ST_Name: Name of the US State (e.g., Alabama).
  • ST_Abbr: Two-letter postal abbreviation of the State (e.g., AL).
  • ST_ID: Numeric State ID (corresponding to the first two digits of the state’s FIPS code).
  • FIPS: Five-digit Federal Information Processing Standards code uniquely identifying the US county.
  • url: URL to the John Hopkins University (JHU) COVID-19 county dashboard.
  • Thumbnail: URL to the JHU dashboard infographic thumbnail.
  • Shape__Area: Geographic area of the county polygon (GIS metric).
  • Shape__Length: Perimeter boundary length of the county polygon (GIS metric).

2. County-Level COVID-19 Summary Statistics

  • Confirmed: Total cumulative confirmed COVID-19 cases in the county.
  • Deaths: Total cumulative COVID-19 deaths in the county.
  • FatalityRa: County Case Fatality Rate (CFR), calculated as (Deaths / Confirmed) * 100 (expressed as a percentage).
  • Confirmedb: Confirmed COVID-19 cases per 100,000 population.
  • DeathsbyPo: COVID-19 deaths per 100,000 population.
  • Recovered: Total cumulative recovered COVID-19 cases (primarily 0 in late-stage data since county-level recovery tracking was widely discontinued).
  • Active: Active COVID-19 cases (primarily 0).
  • DateChecke: Timestamp when the county’s data was last updated (e.g., 03/10/2023 11:05:58).

3. State-Level COVID-19 Metrics

  • State_Conf: State-level total confirmed COVID-19 cases.
  • State_Deat: State-level total COVID-19 deaths.
  • State_Fata: State-level Case Fatality Rate (CFR) percentage (State_Deat / State_Conf * 100).
  • State_Reco: State-level total recovered cases (often 0).
  • State_Test: State-level total COVID-19 tests performed.

4. COVID-19 Policy & Emergency Measures

  • EM_type: Type of emergency order or policy enacted (e.g., "Govt Ordered Community Quarantine").
  • EM_date: The date/time when the emergency measure was officially instituted.
  • EM_notes: Descriptive notes or summary about the emergency order (e.g., "AL Governor issued a Shelter In Place order.").

5. Hospital & Medical Resource Capacity

  • Beds_Licen: Total number of licensed hospital beds in the county.
  • Beds_Staff: Total staffed hospital beds in the county.
  • Beds_ICU: Total staffed ICU (Intensive Care Unit) beds in the county.
  • Ventilator: Number of ventilators available in county healthcare facilities.
  • Inpat_Cap: Hospital inpatient bed capacity.
  • Inpat_Occ: Percentage of inpatient beds currently occupied.
  • Inpat_COV: Average number of inpatient beds occupied by COVID-19 patients.
  • Inp_nonCOV: Average number of inpatient beds occupied by non-COVID-19 patients.
  • ICU_Cap: Intensive Care Unit (ICU) bed capacity.
  • ICU_Occ: Percentage of ICU beds currently occupied.
  • ICU_COV: Average number of ICU beds occupied by COVID-19 patients.
  • ICU_nonCOV: Average number of ICU beds occupied by non-COVID-19 patients.

6. Socioeconomic & Employment Indicators

  • POP_ESTIMA: Estimated population of the county (Census population estimates).
  • POVALL_201: Estimated total number of people of all ages living in poverty (from Census Small Area Income and Poverty Estimates, e.g., 2019/2020).
  • PCTPOVALL_: Percentage of the county population living in poverty.
  • Unemployed: Number of unemployed individuals.
  • Unemployme: County unemployment rate (expressed as a percentage).
  • Median_Hou: Median Household Income in US dollars (USD).
  • Med_HH_Inc: Normalized median household income index, representing county median income relative to a reference baseline (approximately $50,536).

7. Detailed Age Demographics

  • Age_Less15: Population under 15 years old.
  • Age_15_24: Population aged 15 to 24.
  • Age_25_34: Population aged 25 to 34.
  • Age_35_64: Population aged 35 to 64.
  • Age_65_69: Population aged 65 to 69.
  • Age_70_74: Population aged 70 to 74.
  • Age_75_79: Population aged 75 to 79.
  • Age_80_84: Population aged 80 to 84.
  • Age_85: Population aged 85 and older.
  • Age_65_74: Sum of the population aged 65 to 74.
  • Age_Over75: Sum of the population aged 75 and older.
  • AgedPop: Elderly population total (sum of population aged 65 and older).
  • Agetotal: Total population calculated from the age demographic brackets (should equal TotalPop).

8. Racial & Ethnic Demographics

  • TotalPop: Total county population from demographic census records.
  • NonHispWhP: Non-Hispanic White population count.
  • BlackPop: Black / African American population count.
  • AmIndop: American Indian and Alaska Native population count.
  • AsianPop: Asian population count.
  • PacIslPop: Native Hawaiian and Other Pacific Islander population count.
  • OtherPop: Population count of other single races.
  • TwoMorPop: Population count of individuals identifying as two or more races.
  • HispPop: Hispanic/Latino population count (any race).
  • Not_Hisp: Non-Hispanic/Latino population count (all races).
  • Wh_Alone: White population count (alone, including Hispanic White).
  • Bk_Alone: Black/African American population count (alone).
  • AI_Alone: American Indian / Alaska Native population count (alone).
  • As_Alone: Asian population count (alone).
  • NH_Alone: Native Hawaiian and Pacific Islander population count (alone).
  • SO_Alone: Some Other Race population count (alone).
  • Two_More: Two or More Races population count (alone).
  • NonHisp: Mislabeled/Duplicate column. In this dataset, this column exactly mirrors HispPop rather than representing the actual Non-Hispanic population count (which is correctly found in Not_Hisp).

9. Recent Trailing COVID-19 Trends

  • NewCases: New confirmed COVID-19 cases recorded on the last update day.
  • NewDeaths: New COVID-19 deaths recorded on the last update day.
  • Day_1 to Day_14: Trailing 14-day history of daily new case counts leading up to the final update date. Note: Since many states reported weekly, non-zero values typically appear on reporting days spaced exactly 7 days apart (e.g., Day_2 and Day_9).
